# Cattle Lifecycle, Health & Growth Models

## Purpose

Every ranch agent needs to understand the biological clock that drives the entire operation. Cattle aren't inventory — they're living systems with predictable stages, critical windows, and compounding errors. A missed vaccination, a thin cow at calving, a heifer bred too early — these cascade through the production cycle and cost real money.

This document models the **production lifecycle**, **growth curves**, **health protocols**, **nutrition requirements**, and **body condition targets** that every agent should reference when making decisions.

---

## 1. Production Cycle (The Annual Loop)

The cow-calf production cycle is a 365-day loop. Every day off this cycle costs roughly $3-5/day in lost production, or $21/day for every day a calf is born late next year.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    365-DAY PRODUCTION CYCLE                          │
│                                                                     │
│  CALVING ──► LACTATION ──► BREEDING ──► EARLY GESTATION ──►       │
│  (Day 0)    (Day 0-82)     (Day 50-82)   (Day 83-190)              │
│                                                                     │
│  ──► MID-GESTATION ──► LATE GESTATION ──► CALVING                   │
│      (Day 190-250)     (Day 250-283)       (Day 283/0)              │
│                                                                     │
│  WEANING at Day 205-240 (6-8 months)                                │
│  DRY PERIOD: Day 240-283 (45 days before calving)                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Key Numbers

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Estrous cycle | 21 days (18-24 range) | Standing heat 6-18 hours |
| Gestation | 283 days (279-290 range) | ~9.5 months |
| Postpartum anestrus | 45-90 days | BCS-dependent; thin = longer |
| Rebreeding window | Day 50-82 post-calving | Must conceive by Day 82 for 365-day interval |
| Lactation | 205-240 days | Aligns with weaning |
| Puberty (heifers) | 8-18 months | Breed-dependent (see below) |

### Production Phases in Detail

#### Phase 1: Calving & Early Lactation (Day 0-82)

- **Nutrition demand:** PEAK — cow is lactating AND rebuilding reproductive tract
- **BCS target:** 5-6 at calving (1-9 scale); <5 = delayed rebreeding
- **Critical risk:** Dystocia, metritis, delayed cyclicity
- **Agent actions:** Monitor BCS, ensure adequate protein/energy, breeding soundness exams

#### Phase 2: Breeding & Early Gestation (Day 50-190)

- **Nutrition demand:** MODERATE — lactation declining, fetal demand low
- **BCS target:** Maintain 5-6; gain if thin
- **Critical risk:** Early embryonic loss (Days 1-30 highest risk)
- **Agent actions:** Pregnancy check at Day 30-60, bull breeding soundness, AI timing

#### Phase 3: Mid-Gestation (Day 190-250)

- **Nutrition demand:** LOW — dry or low-lactation, fetal growth still moderate
- **BCS target:** Maintain 5-6; this is when you can cheaply add condition
- **Critical risk:** Minimal; good time for routine maintenance
- **Agent actions:** Weaning, preg-check confirmed, body condition scoring

#### Phase 4: Late Gestation (Day 250-283)

- **Nutrition demand:** HIGH — 70% of fetal birth weight gained in final 90 days
- **BCS target:** 5-6 at calving (set during this phase; hard to gain after calving)
- **Critical risk:** Dystocia (oversized calf, poor presentation), milk fever, weak calf syndrome
- **Agent actions:** Increase energy/protein, calving preparation, colostrum management plan

## 3. Body Condition Scoring (BCS)

BCS is the single most useful health metric. It predicts rebreeding success, calf vigor, and feed efficiency. Scored on a 1-9 scale (1 = emaciated, 9 = obese).

### BCS Targets by Production Stage

| Stage | Target BCS | Acceptable Range | Critical Threshold | Consequence if Below |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Calving | 5.0-6.0 | 4.5-6.5 | <4.0 | Delayed rebreeding, weak calf, dystocia |
| Early lactation | 5.0-5.5 | 4.5-6.0 | <4.0 | Extended anestrus, poor milk |
| Breeding (Day 60-82) | 5.0-5.5 | 4.5-6.0 | <4.0 | Low conception, late calving next year |
| Mid-gestation | 5.0-6.0 | 5.0-6.5 | <4.5 | Hard to recover before calving |
| Pre-calving (last 60 days) | 5.5-6.0 | 5.0-6.5 | <5.0 | Weak calf, dystocia, slow rebreed |

### BCS Quick Reference

| Score | Description | Key Visual |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| 3 | Thin | Ribs prominent, no fat over backbone, sharp pins |
| 4 | Borderline | Ribs visible, slight fat over backbone |
| 5 | Moderate | Ribs barely visible, smooth top line |
| 6 | Good | Ribs not visible, smooth fat cover |
| 7 | Fleshy | Noticeable fat over ribs and tailhead |
| 8 | Fat | Blocky appearance, heavy fat deposits |

### BCS → Rebreeding Probability

| BCS at Calving | % Cycling by Day 60 | % Pregnant by Day 90 |
|---------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| ≤4 | 40-50% | 50-60% |
| 5 | 70-80% | 75-85% |
| 6 | 85-95% | 88-95% |
| ≥7 | 90-98% | 90-95% |

**Rule of thumb:** 1 BCS point = ~80 lbs body weight (Angus-type). A cow that drops from BCS 5 to BCS 4 during early lactation has lost ~80 lbs and is 20-30% less likely to rebreed on time.

## 4. Nutrition Requirements by Production Stage

Based on NRC (now NASEM) Nutrient Requirements of Beef Cattle (8th Rev Ed, 2016).

### Cow Requirements (1,200 lb mature weight)

| Stage | TDN (%) | CP (%) | DM Intake (%BW) | NEm (Mcal/lb) | Notes |
|-------|---------|--------|------------------|----------------|-------|
| Early gestation (dry) | 48-52 | 7-8 | 1.8-2.0 | 0.45 | Lowest demand; can use low-quality forage |
| Late gestation (last 60 days) | 55-60 | 9-11 | 2.0-2.2 | 0.55 | Fetal growth + udder development |
| Early lactation (0-60 days) | 62-65 | 11-12 | 2.5-2.8 | 0.64 | PEAK demand; hardest to meet on forage alone |
| Peak lactation (60-90 days) | 60-63 | 10-11 | 2.3-2.5 | 0.60 | Still high; coincide with breeding |
| Mid-lactation (90-180 days) | 55-58 | 9-10 | 2.2-2.4 | 0.52 | Declining demand; match forage quality |
| Weaning/drying off | 48-52 | 7-8 | 1.8-2.0 | 0.45 | Can use low-quality forage again |

### Growing Cattle Requirements (Steers/Heifers)

| Weight (lbs) | ADG Target | TDN (%) | CP (%) | DM Intake (%BW) | Notes |
|--------------|-----------|---------|--------|------------------|-------|
| 400-500 | 2.0 | 65-68 | 12-13 | 2.5-2.8 | Post-weaning growth |
| 500-700 | 2.0-2.5 | 63-67 | 11-12 | 2.4-2.7 | Backgrounding |
| 700-900 | 2.5-3.0 | 65-70 | 10-11 | 2.3-2.5 | Transition to finishing |
| 900-1200 | 3.0-3.5 | 70-75 | 10-11 | 2.0-2.3 | Finishing ration |
| 1200-1400 | 3.5-4.0 | 72-78 | 9-10 | 1.8-2.1 | Final finishing |

### Heifer Development Requirements

| Age | Weight Target | TDN (%) | CP (%) | Key Goal |
|-----|---------------|---------|--------|----------|
| Weaning (205 days) | 55% of mature | 62-65 | 12-13 | Growth without fattening |
| 12 months | 65% of mature | 58-62 | 10-12 | Continue lean growth |
| Breeding (13-15 months) | 60-65% of mature | 58-62 | 10-11 | First conception |
| First calving (24 months) | 85% of mature | 62-65 | 11-12 | Still growing + lactating |

**Critical rule:** Heifers must be 60-65% of mature weight by first breeding. Below 60%, conception rates drop and dystocia risk rises dramatically.

## 5. Health Protocol by Production Stage

### Vaccination Calendar (Cow-Calf Operation)

#### At Birth

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Colostrum | 4 quarts within 2 hours; test Brix ≥22% |
| Navelpole iodine | Dip navelpole in 7% iodine solution |
| Ear tag + record | ID, birth weight, dam, calving ease score |
| Monitor | Nursing within 2 hours, alertness, breathing |

#### 2-4 Weeks

| Vaccine | Type | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Clostridial 7-way or 8-way | Killed | First dose; booster in 2-4 weeks |
| Intranasal IBR/PI3 (optional) | MLV | High-risk calves or early weaning |

#### Branding Age (1-3 months)

| Vaccine | Type | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Clostridial booster | Killed | Complete series |
| Respiratory (IBR/BVD/PI3/BRSV) | MLV or killed | First dose; pre-weaning |

#### Pre-Weaning (4-6 months)

| Vaccine | Type | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Respiratory booster (IBR/BVD/PI3/BRSV) | MLV | 2-4 weeks before weaning |
| Clostridial annual booster | Killed | If due |
| Mannheimia/Pasteurella (optional) | Killed | High-risk operations |

#### Weaning (6-8 months)

| Vaccine | Type | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Respiratory (IBR/BVD/PI3/BRSV) | MLV | Booster at weaning |
| Clostridial | Killed | Annual booster |
| Deworming | — | Strategic based on FEC |
| Implant (steers/heifers not retained) | Growth | Follow label; record lot/date |

#### Yearling Breeding Soundness (12-15 months)

| Vaccine | Type | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Reproductive: Campylobacter (vibrio) | Killed | Bulls + replacement heifers |
| Reproductive: Leptospira 5-way | Killed | Pre-breeding |
| BVD/IBR/PI3/BRSV | MLV | Pre-breeding booster |

#### Pre-Calving (6 weeks before calving)

| Vaccine | Type | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Scours vaccine (E. coli + Rota + Corona) | Killed | Boosts colostral antibodies |
| Clostridial annual | Killed | Maintenance |
| Respiratory annual | MLV or killed | Herd health maintenance |

#### Bull Breeding Soundness Exam (BSE)

| Timing | Exam | Pass Criteria |
|--------|------|----------------|
| 30-60 days pre-breeding | Physical, scrotal circumference, semen motility/morphology | Scrotal circumference ≥34 cm (yearling), ≥36 cm (mature); >70% normal sperm |

### Disease Risk by Stage

| Stage | Top Risks | Economic Impact |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| Neonatal (0-30 days) | Scours, respiratory, hypothermia | $50-200/calf treated; 5-20% mortality |
| Weaning (6-8 months) | BRD, clostridial, coccidiosis | BRD: $500+/case; 2-5% mortality |
| Backgrounding | BRD, internal parasites, pinkeye | BRD: 15-45% morbidity unvaccinated |
| Feedlot | BRD, acidosis, liver abscesses | BRD #1 cause of feedlot death |
| Breeding | Trichomoniasis, vibriosis, leptospirosis | Open cows = no calf = $800-1500 loss |
| Late gestation | Dystocia, pregnancy toxemia, milk fever | Dystocia: $50-500+ per incident |
| Post-calving | Metritis, mastitis, displaced abomasum | Each day open = $3-5; each cycle late = $21-50 |

## 6. Reproductive Model

### Estrous Cycle Detail

| Phase | Duration | Key Events | Management Implication |
|-------|----------|------------|----------------------|
| Proestrus | 2-3 days | Follicle growing, estrogen rising | Watch for early heat signs |
| Estrus (heat) | 6-18 hours | Standing to be mounted | AI 8-12 hours after observed heat |
| Metestrus | 1-2 days | Ovulation (12-18 hrs after estrus onset) | Bleeding may occur; fertilization window |
| Diestrus | 15-18 days | CL producing progesterone | Pregnancy or CL regression |

### Conception Rates

| Method | Conception Rate | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Natural service (good bull) | 85-95% | 1 bull per 25-30 cows |
| AI — single insemination | 55-65% | Timing-critical |
| AI — with estrus sync | 60-70% | Improves timing |
| AI — with fixed-time AI | 50-60% | No heat detection needed |
| Embryo transfer | 40-55% | Recipient quality dependent |

### Pregnancy Loss Cascade

| Stage | Loss Rate | Primary Cause |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Fertilization → Day 14 | 20-30% | Embryonic (genetic, nutritional stress, heat) |
| Day 14 → Day 42 | 5-10% | Failure of maternal recognition |
| Day 42 → Term | 2-5% | Fetal (infection, IBR/BVD, toxic plants) |
| **Overall conception to term** | **60-70%** | **Net pregnancy rate** |

## 8. Key Formulas

### Weight Estimation (No Scale)

```
Live Weight (lbs) = (Heart Girth² × Body Length) / 300
```
Where heart girth and body length are in inches. Accuracy: ±3-5% for BCS 4-7.

### Adjusted 205-Day Weaning Weight

```
Adj 205-Day Wt = ((Actual Wt - Birth Wt) / Age in Days × 205) + Birth Wt + (Age of Dam Adj)
```

| Dam Age | Adjustment (lbs) |
|---------|-----------------|
| 2 years | -60 |
| 3 years | -40 |
| 4 years | -20 |
| 5-10 years | 0 |
| 11+ years | -20 |

### Feed Cost of Gain

```
Cost of Gain = (Feed Cost per Ton) / (lbs Gain per Ton Feed)
```

Example: $200/ton hay, 8 lbs gain per ton DM → $25/lb gain. Feedlot: $300/ton ration, 50 lbs gain per ton → $6/lb gain.

### Break-Even Calculation

```
Break-Even Price = (Total Production Cost) / (Calf Weaning Weight)
```

Typical cow-calf operation: $900-1200/cow/year ÷ 500 lb calf = $1.80-2.40/lb break-even.

## 9. Critical Windows (Don't Miss These)

| Window | Duration | Consequence of Missing |
|--------|----------|----------------------|
| Colostrum (first 6 hours) | 6 hours | Calf immunity failure; 2-3x mortality |
| Estrus detection | 6-18 hours | Missed breeding → 21-day delay |
| BCS at calving | Set 60 days pre-calving | Too thin = no rebreed; too fat = dystocia |
| First 30 days post-conception | 30 days | 20-30% embryonic loss risk |
| Pre-weaning vaccination | 2-4 weeks before weaning | BRD susceptibility spikes at weaning |
| Breeding soundness exam | 30-60 days pre-breeding | Infertile bull = open cows = no calves |

## Sources & Authority

- **NASEM (formerly NRC):** Nutrient Requirements of Beef Cattle, 8th Revised Edition (2016). The gold standard for nutritional models.
- **BCS:** Richards et al. (1986), Beef Improvement Federation guidelines. 1-9 scale.
- **Reproduction:** Beef Reproduction Task Force, University extension consensus. 283-day gestation, 21-day estrous cycle.
- **Vaccination:** MU Extension G2104, OSU Extension E-974, Drovers custom protocol guidelines. Always defer to operation-specific vet protocol.
- **Growth curves:** USDA MARC data, breed association standards. Adjusted 205-day weaning weight is the standard comparison metric.

**Important:** This model provides general benchmarks. Actual targets must be calibrated to breed, region, operation type, and local conditions. Agents should reference this for "is this normal?" decisions and flag deviations to Waylon for operational review.
